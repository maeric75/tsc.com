About Us

Created in 2021, the TREND★SCENDING CHART TOP 100 aims to accurately depict the pop music landscape. We use sales figures, streaming numbers, and radio data to create a points system that dictates a song's placement in the Top 100. Because of how music data is handled and held by a select few companies, our data comes in the form of publicly available estimates provided by Talk of the Charts. The TREND★SCENDING CHART TOP 100 doubles as both a reflection of popular culture and a passion project and academic effort for a university student.

How Does It Work?

The TREND★SCENDING CHART TOP 100 relies on one core formula that dictates the order on the charts:

(Sales/1000)/20 + (Streaming/1000000)*2.25 + (Airplay/1000000)/5

If, however, a song is considered "stale" (see next section), the formula becomes:

(Sales/1000)/20 + (Streaming/1000000)*2.25 + (Airplay/1000000)/10

Radio listeners tend to change the channel when older, less relevant songs come on. This rule reflects that. This formula is adjusted at the end of every chart year to reflect the current listening climate.

Song Status

As mentioned above, a "stale" song has a different formula than others. In general, the formula applied to a song depends on the song's status. There are three statuses a song can have:

  1. Base - The song is currently popular and relevant. No penalties are applied.
  2. Stale - The song is outlasting its popularity. Radio numbers are penalized.
  3. Recurrent - The song is old comparative to its placement. The points it accumulates within a week do not count towards the year-end, but the song is still displayed on the website with a placement determined by its stale score.

A song receives a status based on the number of weeks it has been on the charts and its Hold score, which is the point total a song received on its 20th week. Here are the details:

A song is:

  • Base if Weeks <= 20 or any other condition than below
  • Stale if Base Score < Hold and:
    • Weeks > 20 and Place <= 50
    • Weeks > 36 and Place <= 25
    • Weeks > 52 and Place <= 10
  • Recurrent if Base Score < Hold and:
    • Weeks > 20 and Place > 50
    • Weeks > 36 and Place > 25
    • Weeks > 52 and Place > 10
    • Weeks > 78 and Place > 5 (score rule does not apply)
