Choosin' Texas Holds Strong

May 1, 2026

Ella Langley's rise has been something to behold. After previously peaking at #22 for "weren't for the wind", "Choosin' Texas" rocketed to #1 earlier this year and has not looked back. Now in it's 11th week, it is rivaling other recent, impressive runs at #1:

  • In 2023, Morgan Wallen's "Last Night" set the record, with 22 weeks at the top.
  • Shaboozey's "A Bar Song (Tipsy)" had 12 weeks at #1 in 2024.
  • Last year, "Golden" from the K-POP DEMON HUNTERS soundtrack maintained its peak for 13 weeks.
  • Taylor Swift netted 12 weeks at #1 with "The Fate Of Ophelia" between 2025 and 2026.

Bieberchella Makes Waves

May 1, 2026

Justin Bieber's set from the recent Coachella festival has taken the internet by storm. Fans, nostalgic for better times, streamed his catalog like it was 3012 this week. Here's the damage:

  • Singles from Bieber's 2025 release SWAG rose high ("DAISIES" at #5, "YUKON" peaking at #10).
  • Jumping to #7 is his Nicki Minaj collab, "Beauty and a Beat."
  • "Baby," the song that started it all, enters at #33.
  • "Confident" featuring Chance the Rapper, drops in at #36.

We're "Freakin' Out": Rock Is Back

May 1, 2026

For the first time in recent memory, an authentic rock act has made their way to the Top 40. The breakout hit from Texan rock band Dexter and the Moonrocks, "Freakin' Out," hit #31 this week. It follows alongside other rock or alternative trends this week:

  • Julia Wolf's "In My Room," at #58, similarly harkens back to 2000s rock sounds.
  • Dominic Fike's snarky alternative sound led "Babydoll" to #12 and "White Keys" to #30.
  • Similarly, Malcolm Todd is racing up the charts, with "Earrings" at #27 and "Sweet Boy" at #82.
